Oscilloscope Measure? (Explained)

Writing by Eze Ernest Chibueze | Last Updated On: Jun 1, 2023

An oscilloscope is an essential equipment for building and testing anything dealing with electrical systems and electronics. It is used by most people across different industries, from automotive, aerospace, communication, computer, etc. An oscilloscope has a screen that displays the waveform of an electrical signal, allowing you to analyze further.

So, what does an oscilloscope measure? Primarily, an oscilloscope measures measures voltage. However, some oscilloscopes have advanced measurement tools that can quickly detect a signal’s characteristics such as amplitude, frequency, current, etc.

Generally, an oscilloscope can measure both voltage-based and time-based characteristics. Most modern oscilloscopes do the calculation for you. This means you will find a frequency reading when taking voltage measurements.

If you’re still confused about what an oscilloscope measures, you’ve come to the right place. This article explains all about oscilloscopes, types, what they measure, and why it is important. Stay with us.
